Publishing Intelligence

Know your publishing landscape
before you choose your path.

Based on Jane Friedman's 2025–2026 publishing paths framework. Understanding the terrain is the first step to navigating it with authority.

Big Five & Mid-Size
Traditional publishing · Highest prestige

Publishers take on financial risk and pay an advance. Royalties are paid once the advance earns out. Physical bookstore distribution is nearly assured, with the best chance of mass media reviews. Authors should have mainstream appeal and national print retail potential.

Paid upfront
Agent Required
Almost always
Distribution
National retail
Author Control

Novelists need full MS; nonfiction authors need a strong proposal.

Big Five rarely takes unagented submissions.
3
Agent submits to editors
Can take months; rejections are common even for strong work.
4
Negotiate deal & advance
Publisher handles editing, design, distribution.
5
Launch with full PR support
Bestseller campaigns amplify this significantly.
Best forPlatform-ready nonfiction authors & literary novelists
University & Scholarly
Academic prestige · Peer validation

Examples include University of Chicago Press, Princeton University Press, and Cambridge University Press. Advances are minimal; royalties may run lower than New York publishers. Pricing typically runs high for bookstore placement unless the publisher does books for the consumer market.

Professional & Educational
Wiley, Springer, McGraw Hill · Field credibility

Publishers like Pearson, Wiley, Springer, Elsevier, and McGraw Hill. Advances are minimal; royalties may be modest. Some require the author to assign all rights. They focus on university systems, libraries, and professional organizations. Loss of copyright is a key risk.

Small(er) Presses
Unnamed Press, Coach House, Forest Avenue · Niche reach

A broad category — from Unnamed Press to Rose Metal Press and Microcosm Press. Authors receive a small advance; royalty rates are comparable to bigger houses, but contracts may be more negotiable. These presses prioritize the literary, independent bookstore, and niche communities.

Independent (Self-Publishing)
KDP, IngramSpark, Draft2Digital · Full control

The author retains all rights, all control, and all profit — but also bears all costs and responsibilities. Amazon KDP, IngramSpark, and similar platforms make global distribution accessible. A bestseller campaign can amplify an independently published book just as effectively as a traditionally published one.
